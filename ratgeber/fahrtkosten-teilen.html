Split fuel costs: fair & stress-free

Whether it’s a carpool or a road trip: a clear model avoids discussions. Here are 3 practical models — from “fuel only” to “€/km all-in”.

3 models for splitting costs

Model A: Fuel only (simplest)

Total fuel cost ÷ people

Model B: Fuel + tolls/parking (road trip standard)

(Fuel + tolls + parking) ÷ people

Model C: Agree on €/km (for regular rides)

Kilometres × agreed €/km (e.g. from your cost-per-km calculation)

To get a €/km baseline: Calculate cost per km.

Examples

Example 1: Fuel only

€58.28 fuel ÷ 3 people = €19.43 per person

Example 2: Road trip (fuel + tolls + parking)

(€120 fuel + €25 tolls + €15 parking) ÷ 4 = €40 per person

Example 3: €/km

600 km × €0.23/km = €138 (then split by agreement or pay the driver)

Tips: how to keep it truly fair

  • Decide upfront: model A, B or C
  • For road trips: add tolls/parking to the “pool” right away
  • Driver changes? Split by leg or settle everything at the end
  • Transparency: share fuel receipts or the calculator result

FAQ

Which model is the fairest?
For one-off trips: fuel (A) or fuel+extras (B). For regular rides: €/km (C).

Do we have to include fixed costs?
Not necessarily. If you ride together often, €/km incl. fixed costs is fairer.
